About Me

Hello world! My name is Christopher Altmann. The digital world has fascinated me since I was twelve. It all started with a fourth generation iPod Touch, which gave me access to the Internet. I always tried to bypass the game rules of mobile games and find ways to improve the operating system with a jailbreak. I come from a low-income family, but when I was younger I wasn't able to buy hardware for the latest games, but I still wanted to. This fact has taught me to save and get by with relatively little money.

Since I trained programming and learning at the Brandenburg University of Technology in Cottbus and as a working student in a part-time job, I had taken above-average time for my own personal development. I devour books on the subject of money, which in my opinion is not sufficiently taught. I am excited by the freedom that money makes possible. I realized that money can be learned and that what I have learned will probably save me from many mistakes in the future. That doesn't mean that I am wealthy at the time I write this, but I have a very good orientation on how I will do it. It is the small steps in everyday life that make a difference over the years.

Thanks to teaching at the university, like all my fellow students, I had to go through the hard math modules. The difficult tasks at the university taught me that I can do anything if I want to. I learned discipline and the importance of a healthy habit. The inner attitude (mindset) is the basic requirement to put things into action. Unconscious and conscious beliefs that are absorbed by the environment are the drivers of our actions. When I understood that, my worldview had changed radically. Affirmations help to convert every negative belief into positive. For example, negative beliefs about money do not promote wealth.

I am currently at the point that makes me think about the goal of a value-creating company. However, I notice that I should first master the rules of sales.

Be strong in health, love, finance and experience!
